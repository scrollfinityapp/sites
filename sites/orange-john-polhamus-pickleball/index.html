Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before booking your first session.

How long are lessons and what do they cost?
Sessions typically run 60–90 minutes depending on the program. Private coaching, group clinics, beginner packages, and tournament prep are all offered at different price points. Reach out via email or the contact form for current rates — I'm happy to find an option that fits your schedule and budget.
Do I need to bring my own gear?
If you're just starting out, don't worry — I can point you toward beginner-friendly paddle recommendations and you can often borrow a loaner for your first session. As you progress, having your own paddle makes a big difference. Balls are always provided during sessions.
I've never played before. Is that okay?
Absolutely. The Beginner Fundamentals program is built specifically for players with zero experience. We start from the ground up — proper grip, footwork, serve, and the dink — and make sure every rep is confidence-building. No prior racket sports experience required.
Do you coach kids?
Yes! Pickleball is a fantastic sport for younger players. Lessons can be tailored for kids and teenagers of all skill levels. Group clinics are especially popular for youth players — it's a great way to learn, compete, and make friends on the court.
Where do sessions take place in Arvada?
I coach at several locations around Arvada and the broader Denver metro area, including Apex Simms Street Courts, Apex Center, and Clear Creek Valley Park. Exact location details are confirmed when you book. I'm happy to discuss preferred locations when you reach out.
What is your cancellation policy?
Life happens — I get it. Please give at least 24 hours notice if you need to reschedule or cancel, and we'll find a new time that works. Cancellations with less than 24 hours notice may be subject to a partial session fee. Details will be confirmed at the time of booking.
